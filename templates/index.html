    Feature Reference Guide

    FeatureDescription
    Age (age)Patient age in years
    Sex (sex)1 = Male, 0 = Female
    Chest Pain (cp)Type of chest pain experienced
    Resting BP (trestbps)Blood pressure at rest, in mm Hg
    Cholesterol (chol)Serum cholesterol in mg/dl
    Fasting Sugar (fbs)Blood sugar > 120 mg/dl: 1 = Yes
    Resting ECG (restecg)Electrocardiographic result at rest
    Max Heart Rate (thalach)Peak heart rate achieved during exercise
    Exercise Angina (exang)Chest pain triggered by exercise
    ST Depression (oldpeak)Exercise-induced ST depression vs rest
    ST Slope (slope)Shape of peak ST segment
    Vessels (ca)Major vessels colored by fluoroscopy (0–3)
    Thalassemia (thal)Blood disorder test result

    Category values

    Chest Pain (cp):  0=Typical Angina  1=Atypical  2=Non-Anginal  3=Asymptomatic
ECG (restecg):    0=Normal  1=ST-T Abnormality  2=LV Hypertrophy
ST Slope (slope): 0=Upsloping  1=Flat  2=Downsloping
Thalassemia:      1=Normal  2=Fixed Defect  3=Reversible Defect

    0 — No heart disease
